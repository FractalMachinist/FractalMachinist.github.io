Café Closes, New Faces on the Waterfront
Word around the docks is that the old Pesce-Tarryin’ Café has finally closed its doors. Longtime proprietor Old Man Virello and his daughter have wished Chef Kaz the best in his new venture, noting that “it’s good to see young folks bringing fresh ideas to the waterfront—even if some of us prefer the classics.” Locals say Virello’s daughter is still about town, so perhaps the Pesce-Tarryin’ spirit isn’t gone for good.

Rumor has it the new eatery will feature not only Kaz’s famous fried oysters but also a “secret menu” for those in the know. Some say the old café’s regulars have already staked out a corner table, and bets are being placed on how long it’ll take for the new chef to win over the old guard. Stay tuned for our exclusive taste test next issue!
By Waterfront Correspondent
Lost Locket and Library Rewards
Lost: Silver locket with a blue gem. Sentimental value. Reward offered.
Reward: 100gp per scroll returned. No questions asked. See Marie Petola, Librarium Arcanum.

The locket was last seen near the Ebcansu footbridge, and several children claim to have spotted a magpie with something shiny in its beak. Meanwhile, the Librarium’s reward for lost scrolls has sparked a flurry of activity among local treasure-hunters—one enterprising youth claims to have found a scroll in a rain barrel, though it turned out to be a laundry list.

Music on the Docks This Starday
Open-air concert at the docks this Starday, sponsored by the Royal Mirabilis Academy. Enjoy performances by local bards and student ensembles.

This year’s lineup includes the ever-popular “Salty Shanties” troupe, a debut by the Marshland Minstrels, and a surprise guest rumored to be none other than the elusive bard known as “The Silver Eel.” Vendors will offer fried fish, sweet buns, and the infamous “dockside punch.”
By Royal Mirabilis Academy
Merfolk Mafia Satire: Disguises Gone Wrong
The Merfolk Mafia’s Top 5 Worst Disguises—#3: The “Human” with a tail. Locals say the hat was a nice touch, but the scales gave it away!

Other notable attempts include the “fisherman” who forgot to remove his gills and the “merchant” whose boots left puddles wherever he went. Dockside wags are already taking bets on what the next attempt will be—our money’s on a “seagull” with suspiciously webbed feet.
By Dockside Satirist
Why the Crown Should Invest in Marshland Infrastructure
Improved roads and bridges would boost trade, protect against flooding, and help everyone—landfolk and merfolk alike.

Last season’s storms washed out the main causeway, leaving merchants stranded and fishers grumbling. With proper investment, the city could see safer crossings, more reliable deliveries, and fewer soggy boots. As one dockworker put it, “If the Crown wants our taxes, they’d better build us a bridge that lasts!”
By Local Merchant

Did You Know? The First Bridge of Usalama
Did you know? The first bridge in Usalama was built by a team of dwarven engineers and enchanted by riverfolk mages to withstand the annual floods.

Local legend claims the bridge’s keystone is inscribed with a secret blessing, and that on foggy mornings, you can hear the dwarves’ laughter echoing beneath the planks. Next time you cross, tap the railing twice for luck!

Dockside Recipe: Pickled Kelp & Spicy Mussels
Ingredients: Fresh kelp, mussels, vinegar, red pepper, garlic, salt.
Instructions: Boil mussels until shells open. Slice kelp thin. Mix all ingredients in a jar, cover with vinegar, and let sit three days. Serve with crusty bread and a dash of dockside punch.
Tip: For extra heat, add a pinch of ground marsh pepper.
By Auntie Nessa
