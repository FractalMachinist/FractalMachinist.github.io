Major Research Grants Announced for Marshland Studies
Psyonic Studies Initiative
Up to 80,000🜚 available for research into the origins, applications, and countermeasures of psyonic magic, with emphasis on aquatic and cross-species phenomena. Proposals should explore implications for diplomacy and security.
Underwater Transfiguration Grant
Awards of 45,000🜚 for projects advancing magical transformation techniques for underwater adaptation. Interdisciplinary teams encouraged, especially those addressing artifact interaction or environmental impact.
Paleotechnic Provenance & Interpolity Ramifications Endowment
Major grants (up to 120,000🜚) available for research into the historical, economic, and sociological impacts of ancient artifact recovery. Proposals may address shifts in trade, governance, or intergroup relations resulting from the reintroduction of powerful relics. Studies offering insights for policy or regional cooperation are especially encouraged.
Marsh Flora & Fauna Fellowship
Grants of 250🜚–400🜚 for fieldwork on rare marshland species, magical flora, or sustainable harvesting practices. Open to students and independent researchers.
Merfolk Cultural Exchange Award
Funding of 300🜚 for projects fostering understanding of Merfolk language, traditions, or political structures. Collaborative proposals with Merfolk partners preferred.
Historical Relics Microgrant
Up to 200🜚 for cataloguing, preservation, or public education initiatives related to minor relics or local magical history.
For full guidelines and application deadlines, consult the Royal Mirabilis Academy’s Office of Research Advancement.
